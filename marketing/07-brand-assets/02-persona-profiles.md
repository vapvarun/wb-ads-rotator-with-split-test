# WB Ad Manager - User Persona Profiles

**Purpose:** Guide marketing and product decisions with user understanding
**Use:** Content creation, feature prioritization, messaging focus

---

## Persona 1: The Blogger

### Profile
**Name:** Sarah the Blogger
**Age:** 28-45
**Technical skill:** Intermediate
**WordPress experience:** 2-5 years

### Background
Sarah runs a content blog (food, lifestyle, travel, parenting, etc.). She's built an audience through consistent posting and social media. Now she wants to monetize but doesn't want her site to feel "sell-out."

### Goals
- Earn passive income from her traffic
- Maintain a good reader experience
- Spend time on content, not technical setup
- Track what's working

### Frustrations
- Sidebar ads feel spammy and get ignored
- AdSense is confusing to optimize
- Affiliate links are scattered everywhere with no tracking
- Multiple plugins slow down her site

### How She Finds Us
- WordPress.org plugin search "ad manager"
- Blog posts about monetization
- Recommendations in blogging Facebook groups

### What She Values
- Simple setup
- Non-intrusive ad options
- Clear analytics
- All-in-one solution

### Feature Focus
**Most Used:**
- Image ads
- After-paragraph placement
- AdSense integration
- Affiliate link cloaking

**Might Use:**
- Exit intent popups
- Sticky footer bar
- Email capture

**Unlikely to Use:**
- Classifieds
- Advertiser portal
- BuddyPress integration

### Messaging Approach
- Lead with ease of use
- Emphasize in-content placements
- Show CTR improvement potential
- Position as "set it and forget it"

### Quote
"I just want my blog to earn money without feeling like a billboard."

## Persona 2: The Affiliate Marketer

### Profile
**Name:** Mike the Affiliate Pro
**Age:** 25-40
**Technical skill:** Intermediate to Advanced
**WordPress experience:** 3+ years

### Background
Mike runs one or more affiliate sites (product reviews, comparisons, niche info sites). He makes most of his income from affiliate commissions and takes monetization seriously.

### Goals
- Maximize affiliate revenue
- Track every click for optimization
- Maintain clean, professional URLs
- Catch broken links before losing money

### Frustrations
- Managing hundreds of affiliate links is chaos
- No idea which content drives sales
- Links break without warning
- Existing link plugins lack analytics depth

### How He Finds Us
- Searching for "affiliate link tracker WordPress"
- Affiliate marketing forums and communities
- Comparisons with Pretty Links alternatives

### What He Values
- Robust tracking
- Link health monitoring
- Bulk operations
- Data for optimization

### Feature Focus
**Most Used:**
- Link cloaking
- Click tracking
- Broken link detection
- Bulk import

**Might Use:**
- Display ads (supplemental income)
- A/B testing (Pro)
- Advanced analytics (Pro)

**Unlikely to Use:**
- Classifieds
- Advertiser portal
- BuddyPress integration

### Messaging Approach
- Lead with link management features
- Emphasize tracking and data
- Show broken link detection value
- Position as professional-grade tool

### Quote
"I need to know exactly which links are making money and which are wasting my traffic."

## Persona 3: The Community Owner

### Profile
**Name:** Rachel the Community Builder
**Age:** 30-50
**Technical skill:** Intermediate
**WordPress experience:** 3+ years

### Background
Rachel runs a BuddyPress or BuddyBoss community site (membership, niche interest, professional network). She has an active user base and needs to monetize to cover hosting and development costs.

### Goals
- Generate revenue without alienating members
- Differentiate free and premium tiers
- Keep the community experience clean
- Make ad management hands-off

### Frustrations
- Generic ad plugins don't understand community sites
- Ads disrupt the social experience
- Can't target by membership level
- BuddyPress compatibility is always an afterthought

### How She Finds Us
- BuddyPress/BuddyBoss forums
- Searching for "BuddyPress ads"
- Community building Facebook groups

### What She Values
- Native BuddyPress integration
- User role targeting
- Non-disruptive placements
- Reliability

### Feature Focus
**Most Used:**
- Activity stream ads
- User role targeting
- Logged-in/logged-out targeting

**Might Use:**
- bbPress topic ads
- Profile page ads
- Sticky bars

**Unlikely to Use:**
- Affiliate link tracking (minimal use)
- Classifieds (unless community-specific)

### Messaging Approach
- Lead with BuddyPress/bbPress integration
- Emphasize native activity stream ads
- Show member tier targeting
- Position as community-first solution

### Quote
"My community members are the product. I need to monetize without driving them away."

## Persona 4: The Directory Builder

### Profile
**Name:** David the Directory Owner
**Age:** 35-55
**Technical skill:** Beginner to Intermediate
**WordPress experience:** Variable (might be new to WP)

### Background
David wants to create a local or niche directory/marketplace. He might be starting fresh or converting an existing site. He sees classified listings as a business model.

### Goals
- Build a revenue-generating directory
- Let users self-submit listings
- Charge for premium features
- Minimize day-to-day management

### Frustrations
- Custom directory development is expensive
- Existing classified plugins are clunky
- Monetization features are always extra cost
- Integration with ads is non-existent

### How He Finds Us
- Searching for "WordPress classified plugin"
- Business opportunity content
- Niche marketplace tutorials

### What He Values
- Frontend submission
- Payment integration
- Automatic management
- Revenue potential

### Feature Focus (Pro Required)
**Most Used:**
- Classified marketplace
- Featured/highlighted upgrades
- Wallet system
- Automatic expiration

**Might Use:**
- Display ads (supplemental)
- Advertiser portal

**Unlikely to Use:**
- Affiliate links
- BuddyPress integration (unless combined)

### Messaging Approach
- Lead with passive income potential
- Show ROI calculations
- Emphasize "system runs itself"
- Position as business opportunity

### Quote
"I want to build something that makes money while I sleep."

- Payment automation
- Professional appearance
- Time savings

- Wallet system
- Submission queue
- Advertiser analytics

**Might Use:**
- Display ads (for managed placements)
- A/B testing
- Advanced analytics

**Unlikely to Use:**
- Affiliate links
- Classifieds
- BuddyPress integration

### Messaging Approach
- Lead with time savings
- Emphasize self-service automation
- Show scaling potential
- Position as professional ad platform

### Quote
"I can't grow my ad business if every sale requires hours of my time."

## Persona Usage

### Content Creation
- Write blog posts for each persona's pain points
- Create how-to guides for persona-specific use cases
- Develop case studies featuring each persona type

### Feature Prioritization
- Weight feature requests by persona impact
- Ensure each persona has a complete workflow
- Identify gaps in persona coverage

### Marketing Campaigns
- Segment email lists by persona type
- Create persona-specific landing pages
- Target ads based on persona search terms

### Sales Conversations
- Identify prospect's persona early
- Lead with relevant features
- Use persona-appropriate examples
